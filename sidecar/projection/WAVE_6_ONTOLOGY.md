## 0. The thesis — isomorphism forecloses "right by name, wrong by function"

A **name is a promise about a function.** A test that exercises only the behavior the name *suggests* confirms
the promise's surface, not its substance — so a wrong implementation that *shares the name's surface* passes.
Two live near-misses (recorded here as evidence the danger is real, not rhetorical):

- **`applyDiff`** — by name, "apply the diff to get the target." A name-driven body returns the *stored* target
  and the happy-path round-trip law `apply(between(A,B), A) = B` passes **trivially**. Right by name; wrong by
  function (it ignores its argument). Caught only by the *discriminating* predicate (§7, P-RT₂).
- **"physical column rename"** — by name, "the column's physical name changed." Detected on
  `Column.ColumnName`. Right by name; wrong by function — V2 emits the *logical* name as the physical object
  (§3), so the deployment never uses the physical-name space. Caught only by getting the name-space ontology
  right (§3).

The structural remedy: **make the engine's types and functions isomorphic to the core moves of change (§5).**
When the partition of `CatalogDiff` *is* the set of moves, and each emission channel *is* a move's realization,
and each is pinned by the predicate that *discriminates* the correct function from the plausible one, then a
name cannot drift from its function without breaking the correspondence — which a witness catches. Isomorphism
turns a discipline of vigilance into a property of structure.

**Reified into algebra.** This ontology's entities and laws are cast as the domain's algebra in
**`WAVE_6_ALGEBRA.md`** (the calculus) — *State is a torsor over Delta*; the laws become balanced equations
(the Weyl axioms + a norm + the Project square), reified into `AXIOMS.md` **T12–T16 + A43** with executable
witnesses. Read this ontology for the *interpretation*; read the algebra for the *equation*.

### 1.1 L0 — the five physical facts everything rests on

1. **`sp_rename` mutates the catalog entry in place; the data pages are untouched. `DROP`+`ADD` deallocates and
   reallocates empty pages.** Same logical outcome, opposite physical outcome — one keeps the bytes, one
   destroys them. *Root cause of the rename channel's existence and its separation from the alter channel.*
2. **`ALTER COLUMN` holds a schema-modification lock; a size-of-data change rewrites every row; a widening is
   metadata-only.** *Root cause of "minimum viable touches" as availability, not elegance.*
3. **`NOT NULL`/narrowing is checked against the bytes already on disk and aborts mid-statement on violation.**
   *Root cause of the data-compatibility gate.*
4. **CDC writes one capture row per DML row-version on a tracked table; a full reload re-touches every row.**
   *Root cause of CDC-silence — and (per the premise, §2) of CDC as the ruler of data movement.*
5. **IDENTITY mints independently per database.** The same logical entity has different surrogates in DEV and
   UAT. *Root cause of identity reconciliation and the two-phase sink-minted insert.*

## 2. L1 — the operator's premise (the concrete reality the engine serves)

**The engine is a publication-and-provenance engine for an evolving relational model — not, primarily, a
deployment engine for a live regulated estate.** Its consumers and terminus:

- **An external team's SSIS jobs** map a *legacy application database* into *this* schema's shape, and must
  *track the relational model's evolution over time* to keep their mappings current.
- **Own Dev/QA on-prem databases**, created from the OutSystems Dev/QA cloud databases.
- **The eject** — the terminal state: schema freeze, then the model stops being OutSystems-derived and becomes
  a thing the operator (and the legacy team) owns outright. After the eject there is no OutSystems to
  regenerate from, so the published model and its provenance must be *exactly* right.

**The estate is a lattice of (environment × release-time) cells** — DEV, TEST/QA, UAT, PROD — each the *same
logical estate* (SsKey-stable) realized at a different point in space and time, lagging by releases. Two values
`A` and `B` compared by the engine are **two cells of this lattice** — which is precisely what makes them "two
periodic values": two rhythm-points of one estate.

**The actual flows** (not the generic ones I first assumed):
- OutSystems Dev cloud → Dev on-prem; QA cloud → QA on-prem (extract + load).
- Dev → UAT with **user re-key** (the `UAT-users` command: a full-export whose users are re-keyed to UAT's
  user identities). Forward-only. No masking (Dev/QA data, not sensitive).
- **PROD has no application/data yet** — so PROD-blast-radius reasoning (atomicity, permission gates) is
  *premature*, not central.
- The legacy team consumes the **schema model and its evolution** (not data) via SSIS.

**Cadence:** once a sprint, **demand-driven by the consuming team** — lumpy and batched, not high-frequency.
So the unit of work is "a sprint's worth of model evolution, published for a downstream consumer to map
against and accumulating faithfully toward the eject."

**The why, physically:** OutSystems' own deployment is opaque and platform-controlled (*trust me*). This is a
parallel, SQL-native, repo-tracked, PR-reviewed pipeline so every change is *prove it* — and so the model and
its provenance can be handed to another team and frozen at the eject. "Fidelity as a theorem" is the slogan;
the driver is **auditable, consumable, ejectable control over a model that changes over time.**

## 3. The name-spaces — Identity / Designation / Realization

A thing carries three distinct name-like attributes; they are not interchangeable, and conflating them is the
second near-miss of §0.

| Role | Carrier | Mutability | Answers |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Identity** | `SsKey` | invariant under every rename | *what it is* |
| **Designation** | `Name` (logical) | renames act here | *what we call it* |
| **Realization** | `Column.ColumnName` / `Physical.Table` | derived | *what name the substrate object bears* |

**[policy] `Realization := Designation`** (production default; `LogicalTableEmission`/`LogicalColumnEmission`,
operator-toggleable). V2 emits the logical name *as* the physical object, so the substrate only ever sees the
designation. The `OSUSR_*` physical names belong to a *different regime* (`Realization ≠ Designation`) relevant
only at the one-time cutover — explicitly out of steady-state scope.

**[law] The rename predicate (P-RN).** *A rename operation is required iff, for the same Identity, the
deployed Realization name ≠ the emitted Realization name.* Under the policy this reduces to `deployed.Name ≠
emitted.Name` for the same `SsKey` — which is why renames are detected logical→logical, over deployed-state vs
emitted-state. *Witness:* `RefactorLogEmitter: a column rename produces a SqlSimpleColumn entry`.

**[law] Identity is the match key, never the name (P-ID).** Comparison matches elements by `SsKey` before
comparing anything. *Consequence given the premise:* physical names are not reused **and** provenance compares
*engine-emitted snapshots* (which carry true `SsKey`s, not synthesized-from-name keys) — so name-reuse
ambiguity cannot bite, and endpoint-diff is sufficient (no path-history needed for *disambiguation*; history is
load-bearing for *provenance*, §5 Accumulate). The synthesized-key bound (`AUDIT` §2.3 / 6.A.7) holds in this
estate precisely *because* names are stable and unique.

## 4. The two differentials, the intent filter, and the DacFx seam

**[law] The comparison differential** (`CatalogDiff.between A B`) is *observational*: a description of how two
**states** differ, identity-first (§3 P-ID), modulus-bounded (it sees only the facets it compares — its blind
spots are silent unless named). It asserts nothing about how to get from A to B.

**[law] The transformation differential** (the emission) is *operational*: a **plan** of acts carrying a
*current* substrate to a *desired* one — directional, ordered, substrate-aware, loss-classified.

**[law] The intent filter (P-IF) — the entity the operational zoom reveals.** A *raw observed difference is not
a true delta.* Reading back a substrate and comparing to the emitted model surfaces differences no one intended
— DacFx auto-named constraints, default collations, read-back facet gaps, reformatted computed expressions. A
plan that acts on every observed difference re-emits spurious changes — relocking tables and churning CDC for
non-changes, the exact disease the engine exists to cure. So the pipeline is:

> `observe(deployed, emitted) → raw difference → filter through (tolerance ⊕ operator-intent) → true delta → derive plan`

Getting this wrong by name — "the diff *is* the delta" — is the next trap up the pike. It is why the canary
asserts equality *modulo named tolerances* and why R6 governance is fundamentally a tolerance discipline. It
sharpens pillar 9 to its operational form: **every observed difference is operator-intended (emit) or
substrate-noise (tolerate) — silence on neither.** *Witness floor:* the tolerance taxonomy + canary "modulo
tolerances"; *trigger:* a structured intent/tolerance filter between `between` and the emitters (6.F adjacent).

**[policy] The DacFx-delegation seam.** The emission splits at *who computes the plan*:
- **Schema plan → delegated.** The engine emits the *declarative* artifact (adjusted `CREATE TABLE` + the
  refactorlog); **DacFx computes the schema ALTER at publish.** "Prefer not to emit ALTER statements" — the
  imperative schema ALTER is *not* the deploy artifact.
- **Data plan → engine-owned.** DacFx does not move data; the engine emits the minimal data movement as
  **pre/post-deployment scripts**, measured by CDC (§6).

**[policy] Two emission modes, both first-class:**
- **Fresh replacement** — drop all schema + data, load schema, load data. Always correct, never minimal. The
  safety baseline.
- **Incremental** — adjusted `CREATE TABLE` + **refactorlog appended-and-deduped against the prior refactorlog**
  + pre/post-deployment data scripts. Minimal, CDC-measured. The target mode.

**Repositioning (the §0 self-correction made concrete):** `SchemaMigrationEmitter`'s explicit
`ALTER TABLE … ALTER COLUMN` (6.A.12) is **not** the deploy artifact in this premise. It is the engine's
*internal model of the minimal schema touch* — valuable as a **preview** (what this sprint touches, for the PR
and the SSIS team), a **verification lens** (does DacFx's publish plan match what the engine predicted?), and
an **input** to the data-script generation and CDC measurement. Right by name (it *is* the minimal touch);
re-seated by function (the deploy artifact is declarative + refactorlog + data scripts).

## 5. The core ontological moves — the generators of change (the isomorphism target)

Change over time decomposes into a small closed set of **moves**. The thesis (§0) is that the engine's
structure should be *isomorphic* to this set: the comparison partitions into the moves; each move has one
physical realization, one substrate constraint, one faithfulness class, one discriminating predicate, and one
engine surface. Where a cell reads ⬚ the isomorphism is incomplete — that is the buildable backlog.

| Move | Physical realization | Substrate constraint | Faithfulness class | Engine surface | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Add** (kind/attr appears) | `CREATE` / `ALTER…ADD` (new empty pages) | additive; safe | faithful | `CatalogDiff.Added` → CREATE / `AlterTableAddColumn` | ✅ witnessed |
| **Remove** (disappears) | `DROP` (deallocates pages) | destructive | **refuse** (declared-loss gate) | `CatalogDiff.Removed` → fail-loud refusal | ◑ refused; declared-drop gate ⬚ |
| **Rename** (Designation changes, Identity stable) | `sp_rename` (pages untouched) | **MUST be refactorlog**, never DROP+ADD | faithful (data preserved) | `RefactorLogEmitter` (kind + column) | ✅ witnessed; against-prior ⬚ |
| **Reshape** (a facet changes) | `ALTER COLUMN` (metadata or full rewrite) | type/nullability/length ALTER-able; DEFAULT/computed/identity/PK are not | faithful / lossy-with-warning / refuse | `CatalogDiff.AttributeDiffs.Changed` → DacFx (declarative) / lens (`SchemaMigrationEmitter`) | ✅ lens witnessed; declarative-via-DacFx ⬚ |
| **Reidentify** (surrogate reconciled across substrates) | sink-minted insert + FK re-point | IDENTITY mints per-DB | faithful by named rule, else drop+diagnose | `Transfer` reconciliation (ReconciledByRule / AssignedBySink) | ✅ witnessed (re-key canary) |
| **Move** (data flows, identity-reconciled) | bulk insert + phase-2 FK update | FK ordering; cycles | minimal = CDC-measured; drops fail-loud | `Transfer` + post-deploy data scripts | ✅ fail-loud (6.A.1); CDC-count enforce ⬚ |
| **Accumulate** (history grows) | append snapshot + append refactorlog | refactorlog never deleted; compares to prior | faithful = replay reconstructs | `Lifecycle` (snapshots / evolutionChain / applyDiff·reconstructLatest) + refactorlog-against-prior | ✅ replay witnessed; against-prior ⬚ |

**The disjointness law (P-CH) [law].** The moves *partition* the change — every element of a `CatalogDiff` is
realized by exactly one move, none twice, none dropped. Rename and Reshape are disjoint by construction (a
renamed element carries no shape facet; `CatalogDiff` tracks rename and shape on separate axes), so the
RefactorLog and ALTER channels never touch the same attribute. *Witness:* `migration: a rename alone emits no
ALTER (renames are the RefactorLog channel)`. *Trigger (generalization to all moves at the `migrate`
composition):* 6.D.1.

**The ordering law (P-ORD) [law].** The moves have a dependency order the plan must respect — rename precedes
reshape-on-the-new-name; create precedes insert; schema precedes data. *Witness floor:* DacFx applies
refactorlog before schema ALTERs; `Deploy.executeStream` flushes DDL before bulk. *Trigger:* the `migrate`
orchestrator's explicit ordering (6.D.1).

## 6. CDC as the ruler of minimal movement

**[policy/instrument]** CDC is not a compliance constraint on these deploys (the downstream use is production,
not codebase-critical). It is the operator's **measurement instrument** for *the minimum viable number of data
movements required to arrive at current-A from the provenance of an earlier-A.* The capture-row count in
`cdc.<table>_CT` **is** the data-movement count.

**[law] The data-minimality predicate (P-DM).** *After applying the incremental data plan to earlier-A, the CDC
capture-row count = |true data delta| between earlier-A and target-A.*
- **Floor (|delta| = 0):** idempotent redeploy emits **zero** capture rows. *Witness:* `CdcSilenceTests` +
  `CdcSilenceCrossEmitterTests` (shipped — the change-detecting MERGE predicate).
- **General (|delta| = k):** capture exactly the changed rows, no more. *Trigger:* 6.F.3 — generalize the
  silence canary to assert `capture = k` for a known small delta.

This is the data-side analog of "ALTER not CREATE," and it is where the engine's minimal-touch contribution
*actually ships to production*: schema-minimality is DacFx's given good inputs (§4); **data-minimality is the
engine's change-detection, and CDC is the ruler that makes it observable and enforceable.**

## 7. The entities and their discriminating predicates (the L3 layer)

For each entity, the law that distinguishes correct-by-function from plausible-by-name — the adversarial
witness, not the happy path (§8).

| Entity | Discriminating predicate | Why the happy-path test is insufficient | Witness / Trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Comparison** | **P-CMP [law]:** `isEmpty(between(A,B)) ⟺ A ≈ B over *every* declared facet`. A facet the diff forgets to compare is a silent blindness. | "diff finds the obvious change" passes even when a facet (type, length…) is uncompared — the `isEmpty` trap. | ✅ `CatalogDiff: a column type change surfaces as an attribute-level Changed entry`; ✅ identical-catalogs-empty |
| **Comparison→Plan** | **P-RT₂ [law] (no-cheat):** `∃ base' ≠ source. apply(d, base') ≠ target(d)` — the plan is a *function of its input state*, not a stored result. | the round-trip law `apply(between(A,B),A)=B` is satisfied trivially by `fun _ d -> target d`. | ✅ `applyDiff threads the passed-in catalog, not the recorded target (no-cheat)` |
| **Round-trip** | **P-RT₁ [law]:** `apply(between(A,B), A) ≈ B` modulo the captured surface (stated order-insensitively via `between B result \|> isEmpty`). | direct structural equality over-constrains on un-captured fields + ordering. | ✅ `Time: applyDiff (between A B) A = B (evolution round-trip law)` |
| **Intent-Filter** | **P-IF [law]:** every observed difference is classified operator-intended (emit) or substrate-noise (tolerate); none silent. | a raw diff conflates intent with normalization noise → spurious churn. | floor: canary "modulo tolerances"; ⬚ trigger: structured filter |
| **Channel** | **P-CH [law]:** moves partition the change (disjoint + covering). | "all statements emitted" passes while a rename double-emits as DROP+ADD. | ✅ `migration: a rename alone emits no ALTER`; ⬚ 6.D.1 (all moves) |
| **Plan (rename)** | **P-RN [law]:** §3 — rename iff deployed Realization ≠ emitted Realization for same Identity. | "physical name changed" detects the wrong name-space. | ✅ column-rename SqlSimpleColumn witness |
| **Plan (data)** | **P-DM [law]:** §6 — CDC capture count = \|data delta\|. | "data round-trips" passes while re-touching every row (full reload). | ✅ silence floor; ⬚ 6.F.3 (\|delta\|=k) |
| **Gate** | **P-GATE [law]:** the gate set is *complete* w.r.t. the plan's preconditions — every way the plan can fail at execution has a pre-flight that refuses first. | a plan with an unchecked precondition fails mid-execution (silent zero-rows, half-load). | ⬚ 6.B.1 (data-compat), 6.C.1 (capability) — re-scoped, §10 |
| **Execution** | **P-EXE [target]:** applying the plan is atomic-or-resumable; partial application is impossible or precisely resumable. | a non-transactional load half-populates on failure. | ⬚ 6.C.2 — deferred (PROD empty), §10 |
| **Provenance** | **P-PROV [law]:** the accumulated record (snapshots + append-only refactorlog) *replays* to reconstruct any state from its earlier provenance; entries are never deleted, and new entries are deduped against the prior. | a refactorlog that drops or duplicates entries mis-reconstructs on a fresh deploy. | ✅ `reconstructLatest`; ⬚ 6.F.1 (against-prior) |

## 8. Right-by-function as a method — the red-team, recursed

The five-axis audit red-teamed the *system* (find where the claimed isomorphism is false). **Right-by-function
recurses that method down to each abstraction:** for every type and function, construct the *adversarial
witness* — the input on which a plausible-but-wrong implementation diverges from the correct one — and test
*that*, not the behavior the name leads you to expect.

- Don't test "applyDiff applies" — test that it *threads its argument* (P-RT₂).
- Don't test "the diff finds changes" — test that it *isn't blind to a facet* (P-CMP).
- Don't test "renames emit" — test that they emit *in the name-space the substrate uses* (P-RN) and *don't
  double-emit as a shape change* (P-CH).

The discipline pairs with the existing "Executable AXIOMS via `AxiomTests.fs`": every predicate here earns an
entry that is **the discriminating law**, not a restatement of the name.

## 9. Grounding the totalities — what T-I/T-V/T-VI quantify over

The six totalities (`NORTH_STAR` §3) are predicates; this ontology supplies the entities they range over, so
they stop being slogans:

- **T-I (faithful)** ranges over **Comparison** (P-CMP — complete over its facets) and the **emission functor**

  partiality structure).
- **T-V (orthogonal)** ranges over **Channels** — P-CH (partition) + P-ORD (ordering). The axes compose without
  hidden coupling exactly when the moves partition and order correctly.
- **T-VI (spanning)** ranges over **Gates** — P-GATE (completeness). "Spanning" *is* "the gate set covers every
  precondition the plan's execution requires."

So the climb to L3 (the composed `migrate`) is: make the move-partition complete (§5 ⬚ cells), make the gate
set complete for *this premise* (§10), and compose under P-ORD with P-EXE — all measured by P-DM's ruler.

## 10. Decisions owed + the premise re-prioritization

**Re-prioritization [decided, this premise].** The audit's generic critical path assumed a live regulated PROD.
This premise (PROD empty; external SSIS consumer; provenance + eject; sprint/demand cadence; forward-only +
rekey) reorders it:

- **Up:** Accumulate/Provenance (refactorlog-against-prior; the published, evolving model surface), the two
  emission modes, and data-minimality-by-CDC (P-DM general case). These serve the consumer and the eject.
- **Down (deferred, named):** **6.C.2** (transactional/resumable) and **6.C.1** (permission/connection gates) —
  *trigger:* PROD gains data, or a write-denied environment enters a real flow. Until then, P-EXE/P-GATE for
  PROD are hygiene, not the path.
- **Reposition:** **6.A.12** explicit ALTER → preview/verify/measure lens, not deploy artifact (§4).
- **Holds:** 6.B.1's *live* form is the Dev→UAT rekey compatibility (not PROD tightening); 6.D.1 `migrate`
  remains the composition, gated by data-compat + rekey under P-ORD/P-CH.

**Decisions owed (the three checks — named with trigger; resolve before the dependent slice opens):**
1. **Refactorlog-against-prior input boundary** — is the committed prior `.refactorlog` an *engine input at emit
   time* (engine appends to it, deduping) or a *repo merge-time concern* (engine emits full, repo reconciles)?
   *Gates 6.F.1.*
2. **Provenance rendering for the SSIS consumer** — do they consume the dacpac (CREATE files), a human-readable
   per-sprint changelog, a machine-readable diff, or all three? *Gates 6.F.4 — decides whether `Comparison`

3. **Eject deliverable** — at freeze, is the deliverable the frozen state *plus the full accumulated refactorlog
   history* (legacy team owns the whole provenance), or the frozen state alone (provenance collapsible at
   freeze)? *Decides whether P-PROV is append-forever or collapsible.*

## 12. Addendum — the data leg: how data is coerced to change over time

§§1–11 are the **schema** leg. The **data** leg is the *same shape of change, one plane down*: where the schema
leg moves table/column structure, the data leg moves rows. The whole point of this addendum is that the data
moves are the **row-level analog** of the schema moves (§5), so the ontology stays one instrument across both
planes. The operator's stated preference — *CDC-aware minimum data diff statements over a complete replace* —
is the data-plane reading of "minimum viable touches": touch only the rows that genuinely changed, and let CDC
be the ruler that proves you did.

### 12.0 The schema ∥ data correspondence (the isomorphism, both planes)

| Aspect | Schema leg (§§1–11) | Data leg (this addendum) |
|---|---|---|
| **State** | a `Catalog` (structure) | a table's row-set, keyed by reconciled identity |
| **Comparison** | `CatalogDiff.between A B` | the row diff — `Insert/Delete/Update/Unchanged` per key |
| **Core moves** | Add / Remove / Rename / Reshape / Reidentify / Move / Accumulate | **Insert / Delete / Update / Unchanged(=silence) / Reidentify** |
| **Emission — incremental** | CREATE + refactorlog (→ DacFx ALTER) | **CDC-aware MERGE** (post-deployment script) |
| **Emission — fresh** | drop + CREATE | **TRUNCATE/DELETE-all + bulk reload** |
| **Delegation** | schema ALTER **delegated to DacFx** | **none — 100% engine-owned** (DacFx does not move data) |
| **Provenance / ruler** | append-only refactorlog (+ snapshots) | **the append-only CDC capture log** (`cdc.<table>_CT`) |
| **Intent filter (noise)** | DacFx normalization, auto-named constraints | **empty-string↔NULL, ANSI-padding, decimal scale, collation, computed, surrogate** |
| **Minimality predicate** | ALTER-not-CREATE; `isEmpty diff ⇒ 0 DDL` | **P-DM: CDC capture count = \|true row delta\|; idempotent ⇒ 0** |

The most consequential row is **Delegation.** Schema leans on DacFx to compute the minimal ALTER from declarative
inputs; **the data plane has no such crutch — the engine computes and emits the minimal row delta itself.** So
the data diff is the engine's *hardest-owned* correctness, and CDC is its *only* external ruler.

### 12.1 L0 — the physical floor for data

1. **CDC writes one capture row per row-version change** (`__$operation`: 1=delete, 2=insert, 3/4=update
   before/after). So the capture log *names which move happened to which row* — it is a typed record of data
   change, not a side effect. *Root cause of P-DM and of CDC-as-provenance (§12.6).*
2. **A `WHEN MATCHED THEN UPDATE` with no change predicate updates every matched row** → a capture per matched
   row even when the row is byte-identical. *This is the trap: a MERGE that looks like a minimum-diff (it has
   `WHEN MATCHED`) but captures `|matched|`, not `|changed|`.* The change-detection predicate is what closes it.
3. **A complete replace (TRUNCATE + bulk reload, or DELETE-all + INSERT-all) captures `2 × |table|` rows** (a
   delete per old row + an insert per new) **even if the data is identical.** Correct, but maximally noisy on
   the ruler. *Root cause of the two-mode split (§12.5).*
4. **Surrogate keys mint independently per substrate.** The same logical row is `Id=280` in Dev, `Id=18` in UAT.
   *Root cause of Reidentify and the reconciled-key match (§12.4).*

### 12.2 The data comparison differential — two regimes

Like the schema comparison (§4), the data comparison is observational: classify each row, by reconciled
identity, into `Insert | Delete | Update | Unchanged`. **Where** it is computed splits into two regimes:

- **At-source (engine-computed):** the engine reads both earlier-A and target-A row-sets, computes the delta in
  process, emits explicit `INSERT/UPDATE/DELETE` for exactly the changed rows. Faithful, but requires reading
  *both* datasets (cost scales with both) — best when a prior snapshot is cheap to hold.
- **At-target (MERGE change-detection) — the CDC-aware-minimum default.** The engine emits a `MERGE` carrying
  the *candidate* (target) rows in a staging/`VALUES` source; SQL Server computes the delta *at apply time* via
  the change-detection predicate, writing only the rows that differ. The engine need not know the target's
  current state — the substrate does the diff, and CDC observes exactly the writes. This is what
  `CdcSilenceTests` exercises and what the operator's preference selects.

[policy] **The data plane prefers the at-target MERGE** (the engine emits the *statement that is the diff*, not
the diff it pre-computed) because (a) it needs no read of the live target, (b) its minimality is checked by the
substrate's own equality + CDC, and (c) it is the natural post-deployment-script shape (§4 DacFx seam: data is
engine-owned, emitted as pre/post-deploy scripts).

### 12.3 The core data moves (the row-level analog of §5)

| Move | Realized by | Faithfulness class | Discriminating predicate | Engine surface | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Insert** (key in target, not source) | `WHEN NOT MATCHED BY TARGET THEN INSERT` | additive; safe | P-DM (a genuine new row captures once) | StaticSeeds/Migration MERGE | ✅ witnessed |
| **Update** (key in both, columns differ) | `WHEN MATCHED AND <change-detection> THEN UPDATE` | faithful; **never fires on no-op** | **P-NOOP** (idempotent ⇒ 0 captures) | `changeDetectionPredicate` (null-safe) | ✅ witnessed |
| **Unchanged** (key in both, identical) | *(no clause fires)* | **silence** | P-DM floor (`\|delta\|=0 ⇒ 0`) | the `AND <predicate>` guard | ✅ witnessed (CdcSilence) |
| **Delete** (key in source, not target) | `WHEN NOT MATCHED BY SOURCE [AND scope] THEN DELETE` | **destructive — gated** | **P-DEL-SCOPE** (delete only declared-in-scope rows) | ⬚ scope gate | ◑ gate ⬚ |
| **Reidentify** (surrogate differs across substrates) | reconciled `ON` match + FK re-point (sink-minted, two-phase) | faithful by named rule, else drop+diagnose | **P-REKEY** (relationship preserved modulo surrogate) | `Transfer` reconciliation | ✅ witnessed (re-key canary) |

### 12.4 The CDC-aware minimum-diff MERGE — anatomy (right-by-function)

The deploy artifact for the data leg (incremental mode) is a `MERGE` per table, and its correctness is *not*
"it's a MERGE." Four functional parts, each with a wrong-by-name failure:

1. **The change-detection predicate is the heart.** `WHEN MATCHED AND (<predicate>) THEN UPDATE` where the
   predicate is the **null-safe distinctness** over the updatable columns — per column
   `(Target.c <> Source.c) OR (Target.c IS NULL AND Source.c IS NOT NULL) OR (Target.c IS NOT NULL AND Source.c
   IS NULL)`, OR-folded (the `IS DISTINCT FROM` expansion; `src/Projection.Targets.SSDT/ScriptDomBuild.fs`
   `perColumnChangeDetection`). *Wrong by name:* a naive `Target.c <> Source.c` is `UNKNOWN` when either side is
   NULL → it **misses NULL↔value transitions** (a real change goes uncaptured) *and* an unconditional
   `WHEN MATCHED` captures every matched row. *Discriminating witness:* `Slice γ: CDC-silence … emits zero CDC
   capture rows on idempotent redeploy` + `Slice γ sensitivity: changed-content redeploy DOES fire`.
2. **The `ON` clause keys on reconciled identity, not raw surrogate.** For the Dev→UAT rekey, match on the
   business key (or the post-remap surrogate); a match on the raw surrogate would classify the same logical row
   as `Delete`+`Insert` (surrogates differ by design) — double the captures and a broken relationship.
   *Discriminating witness:* the AssignedBySink re-key canary's `(Order → User-by-email)` join projection.
3. **The comparable-column-set excludes the non-comparable.** The change-detection predicate ranges over
   `updatable columns − {computed (derived), the reconciled surrogate, columns under a declared tolerance}`.
   *Wrong by name:* "compare all columns" fires on a computed column (a function of others) or on the surrogate
   (expected to differ) → spurious captures → P-DM violated.
4. **The `DELETE` clause is gated.** `WHEN NOT MATCHED BY SOURCE THEN DELETE` is the destructive data move
   (analog of the schema drop-refusal). It fires only within a declared scope (a full-refresh of a bounded set),
   never silently across the whole table. *Trigger:* P-DEL-SCOPE (§12.7).

Two-phase realization (deferred-FK / self-referential / cyclic rows) rides the existing `Transfer` machinery
(phase-1 insert with NULLed deferred FKs; phase-2 re-point) under the data ordering law (§12.7 P-ORD-DATA).

### 12.5 The data intent filter — semantic value vs representation noise

The schema intent filter (§4 P-IF) has a data twin: **a raw column difference is not always a true data
change.** Representation noise that must be *tolerated* (or the change is spurious): empty-string↔NULL
conflation (`AUDIT` Data #4 / 6.A.4 — three meanings collapse on `''`), ANSI-padding (`'abc' vs 'abc '`),
decimal scale (`1.0 vs 1.00`), collation-equal-but-byte-different strings. The null-safe distinctness predicate
(§12.4.1) already handles the NULL axis correctly; the *remaining* filter is the empty/NULL + padding/scale/
collation normalization, which must be a **named tolerance** in the comparable-column treatment — emit on a
genuine semantic change, tolerate a representation artifact, **silence on neither.** This is pillar 9 at the
row-cell level. *Status:* the null axis is closed (in the predicate); empty/NULL + padding/scale are ⬚ (6.A.4 +
this addendum's P-DIFF hardening).

### 12.6 CDC as the data-provenance log (parallel to the refactorlog)

The schema leg accumulates provenance in the **append-only refactorlog** (§5 Accumulate); the data leg
accumulates it in the **append-only CDC capture log.** They are the same construct one plane down: a durable,
append-only record of *every move that happened over time*, which is simultaneously (a) the **provenance** — replaying it reconstructs the data evolution from an earlier-A (the data analog of `reconstructLatest`), and
(b) the **ruler** — the operator reads it to verify the deploy moved exactly the minimum. "Arrive at current-A
from the provenance of an earlier-A with the minimum viable data movements" is, precisely: the incremental MERGE
applied to earlier-A produces a CDC log whose row-count = `|true row delta|`. *The CDC log is both the history
and the proof.*

### 12.8 Two modes (data) — both first-class; minimum-diff preferred

- **Complete replace** — TRUNCATE/DELETE-all + bulk reload. Always correct, never minimal (`2×|table|`
  captures). The safety baseline; the fallback when the diff can't be trusted or the table is small.
- **CDC-aware minimum diff** — the change-detecting MERGE (§12.4) → captures `|delta|`, silent on idempotent
  redeploy. The operator's stated preference; the target mode. Selected by the same `EmissionMode` choice as the
  schema leg (`EXECUTION_PLAN.md` 6.F.2) so schema and data switch modes together.
