## 1. The bullseye, re-derived

### 1.1 Why fidelity must be a theorem

Start from the premise, because the premise forces everything. The engine is a **publication-and-provenance
engine for an evolving relational model**, sourced today from an OutSystems estate, publishing schema and data
to on-prem SQL Server and to an external team's SSIS jobs, accumulating an exact replayable provenance of every
change, and **terminating one day at an eject** — after which there is no upstream to re-derive from.

Read that last clause again, because it is the whole reason for the engine's character. OutSystems' own
deployment is opaque and platform-controlled: *trust me*. This engine is the parallel, SQL-native, repo-tracked,
PR-reviewed pipeline that answers *prove it* — and then must hand the proven model and its provenance to another
team and freeze it. When there is no upstream left to regenerate from, "we believe the schema is right" is not a
posture you can hold. The published model and its provenance must be **exactly** right, and the operator must be
able to *see* that they are right without re-auditing by hand.

That is why fidelity cannot be a property a human verifies. A human-verified property degrades to conjecture the
moment the human stops looking, and the eject is precisely the moment everyone stops looking. Fidelity must be a
**theorem the engine proves about itself, continuously** — a checkable state, not an aspiration.

### 1.2 The one law, and why everything is a corollary

The engine's formal soul is a single adjunction between a logical Model and a physical Substrate:

```
Project : Model     ──►  Substrate      (Π — the emit leg)
Ingest  : Substrate ──►  Model          (the reader leg)
Law     : Ingest ∘ Project = identity   (modulo a named, closed erasure set)
```

The phrase *modulo a named, closed erasure set* is the entire ethic compressed to four words. The law is not
"nothing is lost" — some things genuinely cannot survive the round-trip, and pretending otherwise is the lie.
The law is **nothing is lost in silence**: every erasure is a `Tolerance` entry, a structured diagnostic, or a
fail-loud refusal. A silent erasure is strictly worse than no claim, because it manufactures the *illusion* of
fidelity — and the illusion is what the eject cannot afford.

From that one law, every capability falls out as a corollary, which is the reason the system can be both small
and complete:

- **Emitters** (the chorus) are `Project` specialized to a target — they are siblings because they project the
  *same* Model, and they agree (T11) because they project the same keyset.
- **The canary** is the law at runtime: `Ingest ∘ Project = id` checked against an ephemeral database.
- **Drift detection** is the law's failure surfaced: `Ingest(deployed) ≠ Model` is a diff.
- **Remediation** is the law applied to a delta: `Project(Model ⊖ Ingest(deployed))`.
- **Transfer** is the law run across *two* substrates and extended from schema to data.
- **Refactor logs / evolution** are the law applied across *time* — the morphisms between Model versions.
- **The decision overlay** is the law extended to the engine's own *opinions*: a tightened schema, read back,
  must reproduce the decisions that shaped it.

Nine capabilities, one law. This yields the sharpest planning heuristic the engine owns: **a proposed feature
that is not a corollary of the adjunction is probably the wrong feature.** When a slice feels like "build the
reverse of X," the question is "what is X's `Ingest` peer, and does the existing direction-neutral plan already
serve it?" The answer is almost always yes. This is how the engine stays small while its coverage grows, and it
is the rule against which every recommendation in §6 was tested.

### 1.3 The law, lifted: state is a torsor over delta

The engine does not stop at comparing two *states*. Its Wave-6 change algebra lifts the adjunction from states
to the **displacements between them**, and the lift is the most beautiful idea in the codebase:

> **State is a torsor over Delta.** `between A B` (`⊖`) is the displacement from A to B; `applyDiff`
> (`⊕ : State × Delta → State`) is the torsor action; `norm` is the additive metric; and the operative
> minimality measure is physical — **the CDC capture count is the data norm** (a full reload re-touches every
> row, so minimality is not elegance, it is availability). `AXIOMS.md` T12–T16 + A43.

The working gestalt that this buys is exact: **the engine is an accounting system for change.** Identities are
conserved charges; displacements are transactions counted by two independent rulers (CDC for fidelity, `Bench`
for cost); append-only ledgers hold the partial sums (the episode store, the capture journal, the refactorlog);
and the round-trip canaries are the audits. Once you see the engine this way, the whole of §6 reads as a single
instruction: *keep the books balanced, and make the balance checkable.*

### 1.4 The shape of "done": the isomorphism ladder and the six totalities

The bullseye is not "a round-trip test exists." A test can be green while an axis silently erases a feature. So
the target is a **ladder**, climbed per axis:

- **L1 — witness present.** A named round-trip test exists and is live. *(Reached: 5/5 across Schema · Data ·
  Identity · Time · Decision.)*
- **L2 — faithful.** The round-trip loses nothing *silently*: every erasure is named and closed. An axis that
  erases a feature with no surfaced trace is L1-but-not-L2 — and, as §2 and §3 will show, that gap is the single
  most important finding of this whole study.
- **L3 — composed.** The axis is orthogonal (no hidden coupling) and participates in the engine's culminating
  operation: the one-command `migrate A B` (diff → rename → CDC-safe deploy → sink-minted transfer → verify),
  atomic-or-resumable, which refuses loudly rather than corrupting the target.

Six totalities define when the ladder is fully climbed — and they are *predicates*, each with a counterexample
condition, not slogans:

| Totality | The condition of "done" | Where it bites today |
|---|---|---|
| **T-I** round-trip faithfulness | every axis is `Ingest∘Project = id` modulo a **named, closed** erasure | the Decision axis claims faithful with **zero** named erasures (§2.4) |
| **T-II** executable-axiom totality | every axiom has a green witness or a Skip-with-trigger; a gate forbids phantom claims | `citationOf` is a no-op string trail (§2.5) |
| **T-III** input totality | the four-input algebra `Catalog × Policy × Profile × Lifecycle` is complete | Lifecycle is operational; the binding seam is the live edge (§2.6) |
| **T-IV** documentation-from-proof | the coverage map is *generated* from the proof, not authored | half-built: the matrix is generated, the axiom prose is not (§2.5) |
| **T-V** orthogonality | the axes are a basis, not a bundle — no hidden coupling | Identity↔Schema still partly couples at the reader leg (§2.3) |
| **T-VI** spanning | the basis covers the operation's real dimensions | Transactionality/Rollback has **no machine surface at all** (§5.1) |

The unification is the thing to hold: T-I and T-III make the engine *correct on every axis, in both directions,
across time*; T-V and T-VI make those axes a true *basis* that **composes** into the operator's one-command
migration; and T-II and T-IV make the engine able to *prove and describe its own correctness without a human
re-auditing it*. That last clause is the entire reason "replace V1" is a stronger claim than "trust V1."

### 1.5 Where we stand on the ladder

The honest position, reconciled against the code: **L1 is full (5/5). The composed operation exists and runs on
SQL Server.** What remains is the L2/L3 climb — and the gap is narrower and sharper than it looks. It is not a
field of missing features. It is a small, named set of places where the engine **says more than it can show**:
an axis marked faithful that has no detector for its own erasure; a deepest law proven on a *model* of the
substrate rather than the substrate; a guardrail enforced by a bash script rather than a type. The vector from
here to the bullseye is therefore not "build more." It is **make the honest machine see further** — extend the
named surfaces until what the engine claims and what the engine can demonstrate are the same set. That is the
through-line of every section that follows.

## 2. The current nature — a structural decomposition

This is the map of the engine as it actually stands: load-bearing constructs, the algebra already realized, and
the principal tension of each region. It is a portrait an architect could navigate by — strengths *and* debt,
because a love letter that omits the debt is flattery, and this codebase is too good to flatter.

### 2.1 The compiler heart — the IR and the pass algebra

The core is two interlocking algebras. The **Catalog IR** is a four-level nested coproduct
(`Catalog → Module list → Kind list → (Attribute|Reference|Index|ModalityMark) list`, `src/Projection.Core/Catalog.fs`)
where identity is `SsKey` everywhere, equality-by-identity is a named function (`Kind.byIdentity`, A4), and the
aggregate root `Catalog.create` rejects dangling FKs, illegal trust-quadrants, and storage-type mismatches in
*one fused walk* — so the structural invariants are unforgeable. It is value-pure yet O(1)-indexed via
per-instance `ConditionalWeakTable` caches: purity without the linear-scan tax.

The **passes** are arrows in a Kleisli category over a WriterT-stacked dual writer:
`Pass<'a,'b> = 'a -> Lineage<Diagnostics<'b>>` (`Diagnostics.fs`), with `id`, `compose` (`>=>`), and
`composeAll` whose left/right-identity and associativity laws are *theorems over the bind*, property-tested. The
entire Core pipeline is **one value** — `RegisteredTransforms.chainSteps : ChainStep list` — from which both the
metadata registry and the execution chain *project*, so `transform.registered` provably equals what runs and the
three-parallel-list drift the design once suffered is structurally impossible. This is the single most important
architectural fact in the codebase: the pass chain *is* the composed operation, not a description of it.

The algebra already realized here is deep: a Kleisli category, a WriterT monad-transformer stack over a product
monoid, total lenses with the three optic laws, the nested-coproduct module decomposition (T2), endofunctor
passes (A19), a fan-out combinator (`Composition.fanOut`) that is morally a distributive law, and reified
mutation as a derive-macro (`LineageBuffer.Buffer`: private ctor + `RequireQualifiedAccess` makes the mutation
invisible from the type plane).

**The principal tension** is the `ComposeState` open-product: it grows one `Option` field + one `with*` setter
*per analytic pass* (12 today), and the lift surface has four near-identical `lift*` functions plus two bespoke
inline `ChainStep` literals for the passes that did not fit a builder. Each new analytics pass costs a record
field, a setter, and a lift shape — a linear tax that will compound exactly as the basis (T-VI) gains its
missing dimensions. This is the natural home of a future typed evidence-map, *but that abstraction has not earned
its second shape yet* (§6 keeps it deferred).

### 2.2 The sibling Π emitters and the typed-AST discipline

`Project` is realized as a family of sibling emitters that all consume a subset of `Catalog × Profile` —
**never Policy** (A18, structurally enforced: the `Emitter<'element>` alias *cannot name* `Policy`) — and
produce one of two canonical description forms: an indexed `ArtifactByKind<'element>` (T11 keyset-by-
construction, made unforgeable by the private-ctor derive-macro) or a flat `seq<Statement>` (A35, the typed
deterministic statement stream whose realization — bulk, text, parallel — is invisible to Π). Strings genuinely
emerge only at terminal boundaries: `Sql160ScriptGenerator.GenerateScript`, `Utf8JsonWriter`. The `Statement`
DU's `ALTER*` variants are the emitter-side realization of the change algebra — `Project` specialized to a
`CatalogDiff` displacement, the migration leg of the composed operation.

> **A count to correct, once, for the record.** There are **seven** emitter targets, not six. The often-cited
> "six siblings" omits `Projection.Targets.OperationalDiagnostics` (`RemediationEmitter`, `DecisionLogEmitter`,
> `SuggestConfigEmitter`, `ActionableDiagnostics`, `Routing`) — which is precisely where two of the nine
> corollaries live as artifacts: *remediation = the law on a delta* and *the decision overlay = the law on the
> engine's own opinions*. `DecisionLogEmitter` already emits the decision overlay as an operator artifact; this
> matters enormously for §2.4, because it means the Decision axis's **emit** half is built and shipped — it is
> only the **readback** half that is missing.

**The principal tension** is `ConstraintFormatter` (`Projection.Targets.SSDT/ConstraintFormatter.fs`): the one
place the codebase violates its own first pillar. It re-parses ScriptDom's *already-emitted* SQL line-by-line
with `IndexOf`/`Substring`/`Split` to reshape constraints into V1's elegant form — a string→string transform
sitting *after* the typed AST, throwing the typed structure away and recovering it by string surgery. The file's
own header concedes that subclassing the generator was "considered and rejected (visibility lift cost too high)."
This is the single largest gap in the typed-AST claim, and it is debt of exactly the kind the house names a
failure mode (text-builder-as-first-instinct, inverted). Two smaller siblings of the same tension: the JSON
`Utf8JsonWriter → MemoryStream → byte[] → JsonNode.Parse` dance duplicated verbatim across four-plus sites with
no shared seam, and `DataInsertScript` storing both typed rows *and* their pre-rendered strings (the description
and its interpretation materialized side-by-side, half-defeating A35).

### 2.3 The reader leg and the movement engine (the adjunction made operational)

This is the `Ingest` leg made executable and the change algebra made to run. The reader leg is `ReadSide.read`
(INFORMATION_SCHEMA/`sys.*` → a `Catalog`). The differential is a clean two-layer split that the ontology names
precisely: the **observational** differential `CatalogDiff.between : Catalog → Catalog → CatalogDiff` partitions
`source ∪ target` SsKeys into four pairwise-disjoint move classes and descends through five channels; the
**operational** differential (the emission) is a *plan* of acts carrying a current substrate to a desired one —
directional, ordered, substrate-aware, loss-classified. Between them sits the **intent filter**: a raw observed
difference (a DacFx auto-named constraint, a default collation) is not a true delta, and the engine knows it.

The algebra realized here is the richest in the codebase: a **groupoid/torsor** on `CatalogDiff` (`compose` is
partial composition typed by endpoints — `None` on a non-adjacent pair, fail-loud, never silently wrong; `norm`
is the metric; `applyDiff` is the action); a parameterized-lookup harmonization (A40) where `remapRowFksWith` is
*one* algorithm with the assignment store as its axis; a surjection from operator user-match choices onto one
reconcile machinery; and a ledger-of-replay in `CaptureJournal`. With the movement engine done, `migrate A B`
exists, runs on SQL Server, and composes the whole arc — the T16 master equation is a live witness.

**The principal tensions** are three. First, **Schema and Identity still couple at the reader leg** (a T-V
violation): `ReadSide` synthesizes attribute/kind SsKeys from physical coordinates, so on a non-V2-authored
source a rename changes the key and lands in `Removed + Added` rather than `Renamed`. This is real — but §3.5
will show the *fix* the chorus first proposed for it was wrong; the genuine residue is at the attribute grain
only. Second, **two near-identical write engines** (`writePlan` materialized-with-reconcile vs
`writePlanStreaming` bounded-memory-no-reconcile) duplicate the two-phase orchestration across row carriers — a
clean compression candidate, and the reason the streaming arm cannot yet offer the sink-untouched guarantee
(NM-31 — **closed 2026-06-15**; the streaming arm now has a reconcile leg, see the Reconciliation addendum). Third, **`compose` recomputes `between` twice**, discarding the channel structure it already holds —
an O(N log N) re-derivation where a true torsor `+` would patch the channels (§4 picks this up).

### 2.4 Policy, the decision algebra, and the silent fifth column

Policy is a five-axis orthogonal product (`Selection · Emission · Insertion · Tightening · UserMatching`, A12
amended) whose neutral element `Policy.empty` is a first-class input and whose composition is two homomorphic
monoids (`merge`, and the `PolicyExpr` combinator DSL with a structure-preserving `eval`). The Tightening axis
is not a mode enum but a *registry of named interventions* (`Nullability | UniqueIndex | ForeignKey |
CategoricalUniqueness`), each a thin `Composition.fanOut` config — the traversal collapsed, the strategies
injected. The algebra is genuinely rich: a commutative monoid, a free term algebra with an evaluation
homomorphism, a bounded lattice on Selection with a De Morgan duality, a graded/versioned object
(`VersionedPolicy` with a SemVer grade and a content digest), and smart-constructor refinement types
(`NullBudget ∈ [0,1]`).

**The principal tension is the most important single finding in this document, so it gets named precisely.**
*The decision adjunction is not read back.* The SSDT emitter consumes all four overlay sets and emits
`WITH NOCHECK` (untrusted FK) and `UNIQUE` (promoted index) from the `DecisionOverlay`. The reader leg *already*
recovers the trust bit (`ReadSide` reads `sys.foreign_keys.is_not_trusted` and threads it into the Catalog as
`Reference.IsConstraintTrusted`, `ReadSide.fs:1171`). But the engine's **general comparison surface is
decision-blind**: `PhysicalForeignKey` (`PhysicalSchema.fs:153`) is a six-field record with **no trust field**;
`PhysicalSchema.toPhysicalIndexes` reads the *catalog's* uniqueness, not the overlay's; and
`PhysicalSchema.ofCatalog` never takes a `DecisionOverlay` at all. So a `NoCheckFk` or `EnforceUnique` decision
is emitted into DDL, survives the deploy, is recovered by `ReadSide` — and then **discarded at the diff
boundary**, which is the only comparator the live canary runs.

And here is the sharp edge. The matrix's honesty mechanism — *an axis can be capped at L2-partial only if a
`ToleratedDivergence` tagged for that axis exists* — is **structurally inert on the Decision axis**, because all
eight tolerances tag only Schema or Data (`Tolerance.fs`). There is no Decision-axis tolerance variant. So
"Decision ✅ faithful / ✅ L3" is not *proven* — it is **unfalsifiable by construction**: the machine has no way
to mark it partial. The engine, whose entire credibility rests on "the generator under-claims, never
over-claims," over-claims here — on three of five axes (Decision, Identity, Time have *zero* tolerances), the
under-claiming guarantee is vacuous. This is not a hidden bug; the matrix footer itself admits the "3-axis
Decision adjunction" is an "unwitnessed sub-axis." The gap between that admission *in prose* and the tolerance
set *in code* is the gap this document most wants closed.

Two smaller tensions: three structurally-dead FK config bits survive on a "V1 parity" justification that Pillar
6 arguably forbids (the dead-algebra-retirement precedent says delete zero-consumer algebra), and
`VersionedPolicy.digestOf` uses `sprintf "%A"` as its canonical representation — determinism-by-luck, in a
corpus whose whole digest discipline (`TransformRegistry.digest`) is painstaking length-prefixed determinism-by-
construction.

### 2.5 The self-verification machine — and where the honesty is reachable-but-unreached

The engine's epistemic spine is rare and real: a self-report that **structurally under-claims** (L1/L3 require a
witness test to physically exist; L2 requires an `OpenGap` tolerance to be deleted from source) plus a pure-bash
honesty gate (`verifiability-gate.sh`) that makes "a `Skip` entry claiming Bucket A/B" a build failure — closing
the historical phantom-coverage defect. `matrix-status.sh` regenerates the L1/L2/L3 matrix from exactly two
proof surfaces, and `MatrixLadderTests` pins the generator's keystone. The algebra here is elegant: the
adjunction-as-test, Tolerance as a Galois/quotient erasure set, a partial lattice under set-inclusion, and
Skip-stubs-as-a-deferral-monad (every deferred proof carries its bucket and its promotion trigger).

**The principal tension** is that the honesty is bounded by the *reach* of the named surfaces, and three
boundaries are crossable today. First, `citationOf` is `ignore (testFilePath, testName)` — **112 citations are a
pure string trail with no compile-time or runtime existence check** (verified on disk: 112 sites, 42 Skip
stubs). A renamed or deleted cited test leaves the axiom green; the comment admits existence was grep-verified
only at first commit. This is the named failure mode "performance-of-compliance" (an audit trail shaped like
proof) lurking in the heart of the proof machine. Second, the L1/L3 witness binding is a *substring* grep, not a
structural by-Name binding. Third, T-IV is half-built: the matrix is generated, but `AXIOMS.md` /
`PRODUCT_AXIOMS.md` prose is hand-maintained by a chapter-close ritual — the gate's own header names "generate
the L3 surfaces FROM `AxiomTests.fs`" as the way to "close the phantom class for good," and it is unbuilt.

A related correction the synthesis owes its reader: the **writer "trinity"** (`Lineage` / `LineageTree` /
`Certificate`) is cited across the doc corpus as architecture, but only `Lineage` (the linear writer) exists in
`src/`. `LineageTree` (branching free monad) and `Certificate` (terminal) are **documentation horizon, not
code**. By the dead-algebra discipline, doc-only algebra with zero code consumers should be marked aspirational,
not described as built — and a recommendation that proposes a "first consumer" for `LineageTree` violates
evidence-gating, because there is no algebra there to consume.

### 2.6 The orchestration, config control-plane, and the operator instrument

Runs are bracketed by `RunEnvelope.bracket` (fresh runId, mandatory terminal `summary.runComplete`), and the
stage arc is a typed program over `RunSpine` driven by a `staged spine { }` CE — a free-monad-flavored program
over time that guarantees `declared ⇔ executed ∪ aborted` (an open stage at run-end becomes a *named* Aborted,
not a board hang). This is the writer-monad trinity's *temporal* sibling, and it is lovely. Config flows through
nine `*Binding` modules (each a Kleisli arrow `Config.<Section> → Result<CoreType>` over the validation
applicative), and the operator surface is a clean functor `Code → Copy → Surface → View` where the View is a
coalgebra rendered three ways (pretty, plain, JSON) that can never drift. A44 names the control-plane adjunction:
`render ∘ resolve = id` on the config⟷spec pair — the engine's own adjunction lifted to the operator's hands.

**The principal tension** is duplication-by-convention: the nine `*Binding` modules re-implement the same
opt-in-gate + `bindError` + `aggregate` skeleton with a hand-rolled four-way `Ok/Error` applicative match, and —
more dangerously — the binding *convergence* is written three times (a 4-Result tuple in `runWithConfigCore`, a
3-Result tuple in each of two shaped-catalog runners), free to drift on which bindings each path threads. This
is the exact seam `THE_CONFIG_CONTROL_PLANE.md §6` flags as riskiest (`applyModuleFilter` on the model-read path
but not the live/docker path). And A44 itself is enforced by convention + scattered sweeps, not by one closed-DU
reachability witness analogous to `CapabilitySurveyTotalityTests`.

### 2.7 Identity, the registries, and the scalar primitives

This is the conserved-charge layer. `SsKey` is a closed four-variant DU
(`OssysOriginal | Synthesized | DerivedFrom | V1Mapped`) whose variant tag carries A1's faithfulness bound
*type-visibly* — unconditional for GUID roots, bounded for name-synthesized roots — and a recursive length-
prefixed serializer makes it a recoverable on-disk identity. Three orthogonal name-spaces sit side by side and
are kept apart *by type*: **Identity** (`SsKey`), **Designation** (`Name`, presentation-only, where renames
act), **Realization** (the physical object's name). The algebra is a textbook of good practice: codec adjunctions
with round-trip laws (`serialize ⊣ deserialize`, injective via length-prefixing), a Galois-shaped semantic↔
concrete pair (`PrimitiveType` ⇄ `SqlStorageType`), orientation newtypes as a phantom-typed torsor guard
(`SourceKey`/`AssignedKey`), and the private-ctor derive-macro on `Verified<'entry>`.

**The principal tensions** are small and local: `SourceKey`/`AssignedKey` are bare `of string` with no smart
constructor while their user-id siblings wrap a validated `UserId`; `TableId.Catalog` degrades to `string
option` (the Realization name-space is type-complete on schema/table/column but stringly-typed on the catalog
axis); and the registry knows *which axis* a transform touches but not *which identities*, so identity-level and
transform-level provenance are two unjoined tables.

### 2.8 The testing surface — the engine's self-knowledge organ

The suite's deepest move is that **proof and proof-claim live in one gated artifact**: `AxiomTests.fs` is a
literal coverage map, and the bash gate makes it unable to lie about its own coverage. Around that spine sits a
clear taxonomy — totality tests (`required ⇔ surveyed`, `code ⇔ copy`, `registered ⇔ executed`, the same
bidirectional-subset proof instantiated four times), the adjunction law realized in-process, codec isomorphisms
over a constructed-valid generator, and intentional-fail probes that prove each totality property actually
catches its named failure mode.

**The principal tension** is the Fact:Property ratio (~24:1) — example-testing dominates, FsCheck is concentrated
in ~14 files, and the youngest layer (the Wave-6 change algebra T12–T16) is the *least* swept: the T16 master
equation `applyTo (plan A B) A = B` is cited to a single hand-built fixture, and `genCatalogPair` does not exist.
And the deepest law's strongest proof is in-process, not real-wire: `AdjunctionLawTests` sweeps `(Columns,FKs)`
via an in-process inverse leg; the true round-trip is a single Docker fixture with the full sweep deferred. **With
the reverse-leg engine done, that deferral's trigger has fired** — and it is the highest-value unfired proof in
the system (§5.2).

### 2.9 Five dimensions the first pass missed — the engine auditing its own audit

A completeness pass over the decomposition surfaced five whole dimensions the structural map had not named.
Recording them is itself in the spirit of the engine — the audit that finds what the audit missed.

- **Concurrency / parallel deployment.** `TopologicalOrder.fs:153` defines `ParallelSafe<'a>` — a private-ctor
  derive-macro whose docstring says "the comment-borne MUST dies, the type lives," minted only by
  `TopologicalOrder.levels` and consumed by `Deploy.executeBatchParallel` (real `SemaphoreSlim`-bounded fan-out
  with DMV-probed parallelism and a pool cap). It is arguably the *cleanest* example in the codebase of a MUST-
  comment becoming a type theorem, it governs the live deploy hot path, and it raises a real open question: is
  within-level-parallel deploy **outcome-deterministic** regardless of completion order? The `ParallelSafe`
  token asserts independence; nothing cited proves the canary's `B'≡B` holds under reordered execution.
- **Cost / observability.** `Bench` (`Bench.fs:103`) is the *one* sanctioned module-level mutable — a global
  `Dictionary` under `lock` — and therefore the most load-bearing impurity in the system. It is hammered
  concurrently by `executeBatchParallel`, it is deliberately T1-exempt (the one non-deterministic production
  value), and the perf-gate is its regression fitness function. Pillar 7's "every hot path has `Bench.scope`" is
  one of the eight supreme disciplines; the cost dimension is where the estate-scale transfer lives or dies.
- **Security / trust boundary.** The engine's premise is *ingesting an untrusted external estate* and writing to
  SQL Server with elevated rights — a three-layer injection defense (typed `Coordinates` exclude hostile input
  structurally; `Identifier.EncodeIdentifier` quotes; parameters bind) that is a genuine, uncredited strength,
  paired with an unaudited credential-flow risk (passwords through `DockerImageEmitter`, connection strings
  through the Voice/journal/`Bench`). "The estate is untrusted input" is a load-bearing assumption no axiom
  encodes.
- **Failure-mode / retry.** `Projection.Adapters.OssysSql/Retry.fs` is a Polly transient-classification layer —
  and it documents a *named un-retried gap*: mid-stream transients after the reader opens cannot be retried, "left
  un-retried for this slice." On the now-shipped streaming reverse leg at estate scale, that is a real resilience
  hole exactly where a transient is most likely and most expensive — with `CaptureJournal` sitting right there as
  a latent checkpoint-resume mitigation.
- **Persisted-state evolution (Time, applied to the engine's *own* durable state).** The engine accumulates
  replayable provenance in durable NDJSON stores. Several high-leverage moves change a *serialized form*
  (`ConstraintState`, the persisted digest, the SsKey basis). There is a torsor on Catalog deltas — but is there
  any notion of a *store-schema* delta? The one extant discipline (NM-34: a missing field reads as `None`) is the
  de-facto store-codec contract, and it deserves to be named as such and made a gating checklist, lest the
  amelioration corrupt the very provenance the engine exists to preserve.

These five are not gaps to be filled this quarter. They are the dimensions a complete planning document must
*hold in view*, and §6/§7 sequence the moves that touch them with the right gates.

## 3. Parallel derivations — the chorus of lenses

The study derived improvement methodologies through nine independent lenses. The point of nine lenses is not
nine answers; it is to see whether the *same* answers arrive through unrelated doors. They did — and the pattern
of convergence is itself a finding, because a recommendation that one lens reaches is an opinion, a recommendation
that five lenses reach independently is a structural fact about the codebase, and a recommendation that five
lenses reach *because they copied the same surface observation* is an echo-chamber that must be caught. All three
happened. Here is the chorus, distilled to its thesis and its highest-leverage moves, with the adversarial
verdict folded in.

### 3.1 The nine theses

| Lens | Central thesis |
|---|---|
| **Domain & ubiquitous language** | The engine is, at the type level, one of the most domain-faithful F# codebases imaginable — the gaps are *second-order*: the operator's move alphabet and protein-workflow catalog live only in prose, so the same move wears different names across modules (`Reshaped ↔ Changed`, `Insert/Update ↔ Phase1Merges/Phase2Updates`). |
| **Epistemic (the knowing machine)** | The honesty is genuinely rare but **bounded by the reach of the named surfaces**; three boundaries are crossable today, and each converts an *asserted* cell into a *showable* one — chiefly the Decision-axis readback and the `citationOf` no-op. |
| **Testable surface (the ladder)** | Each over-claim in the matrix maps to a *specific* missing green test; the work is to turn each into either a readback property or a named `OpenGap` tolerance, and to replace fixture-bound laws with generator sweeps. |
| **F# idiom & library leverage** | The high-leverage moves are **not new abstractions** — they are *finishing* idioms and libraries the codebase already sanctioned but half-adopted (FsToolkit `validation { }` at the bindings; retiring the one `sprintf "%A"` digest; a shared JSON-writer seam). |
| **Advanced algebra** | "More algebra" pays rent in exactly four narrow half-realized places (the diff `ChannelSpec`, the triangle inequality as a named law, the vestigial `Result` on `between`, a `Traversal` optic) — and several *tempting* category-theory moves are correctly **dead**. |
| **IR compression** | The engine keeps collapsing the **traversal** while leaving the **descriptor** duplicated; the move is to push each already-discovered intermediate shape one level up — "the descriptor IS data, traversed once." |
| **Architectural fitness** | The hexagon is *real* in the dependency graph, but its guardrails are an out-of-band bash+grep tier; convert the standing commitments into **in-assembly fitness functions** so the architecture cannot silently rot. |
| **Creativity & scaffolds** | The movement engine's completion unlocks not a new headline operation but the **closure of half-built provenance loops** into operator-facing corollaries (a machine-queryable change-manifest; the decision plane made recorded). |
| **Code quality & primitives** | The remaining quality surface is a *short, named* list of sites where an invariant is enforced by convention when it could be a **type theorem** — chiefly the `(HasDbConstraint, IsConstraintTrusted)` quadrant. |

### 3.2 Where the lenses converge — and what the convergence means

**The strongest convergence: the Decision-axis readback.** Five lenses (epistemic, testable-surface, code-
quality, domain, and both inverse-space and opportunistic scouts) arrived independently at the same keystone —
the FK-trust and uniqueness-promotion decisions are emitted but never read back, and the comparison surface
(`PhysicalForeignKey`) has no field to carry the decision. The adversarial layer confirmed this is the **best-
grounded set in the whole catalog**: every cited line checks out, and crucially *the read leg is already free*
(`ReadSide` recovers `is_not_trusted` today), so this is a **true uncashed corollary, not a new feature**. When
five unrelated analytical stances and the ground truth all point at one ~M-sized move, that move is not an
opinion. It is the next facet to cut. It is the spine of §6 and the first real cash of §7.

**The second convergence: the descriptor-is-data compression.** The algebra lens and the IR-compression lens
arrived from opposite directions — one counting categorical structures, one counting duplicated source — at the
same observation: the four diff channels are byte-identical modulo their entity accessors (the code's own comment
says "mirrors `attributeDiff` EXACTLY"), so a single `ChannelSpec` value collapses ~8 functions and 4 fold-blocks
into 4 small spec values. Convergence from a structural lens and a mechanical lens on one site is a strong signal
that the abstraction is *real* (it pays rent in collapsed code), not speculative.

**The third convergence: convention → type theorem.** The architectural-fitness, code-quality, and epistemic
lenses all, in their own vocabularies, said the same thing: the engine's standard is "make the invariant
structural" (the private-ctor derive-macro, the closed-DU totality, the smart constructor) — and there is a short
list of load-bearing invariants currently held by *convention* (a grep rule, a runtime predicate, a `sprintf`
digest, a no-op citation) that have not yet been brought to that standard. The amelioration is to hold those
sites to the codebase's *own* bar.

### 3.3 Where the lenses pull in tension — the real design choices

A tension between lenses is not noise; it names a genuine choice the architect must make. Two are worth stating.

**Compression vs. evidence-gating.** The IR-compression and domain lenses *want* to unify the four enumerations
of the move alphabet (`ChannelDiff` / `SchemaLoss` / `Statement` / `ChannelCounts`) into one closed `SchemaMove`
DU that the diff, the loss-gate, the emitter, and the norm all project from. It is the most elegant move on the
board and the one that would make "the seven schema moves" a *literal type* rather than a doc claim. The
discipline lens — and the adversarial reviewers — **killed it for now**, and they were right: the four
enumerations *agree today*, so unifying them is speculative abstraction with the highest blast radius in the
catalog and no fired forcing function. The synthesis honors both: do the **zero-risk first step** now (rename
`ChannelDiff.Changed → Reshaped` so the diff and migration layers speak one word), and keep the full basis behind
the trigger of an actual divergence. This is the codebase's deepest discipline in miniature — *IR grows under
evidence* — and watching the most beautiful idea get correctly deferred is the clearest proof the discipline is
real.

**Faithfulness vs. designed boundaries.** Four analyses converged on "ReadSide synthesizes SsKeys with a flat
basis while OSSYS uses typed segments — a faithfulness leak; switch it to `synthesizedComposite`." This is the
**echo-chamber the completeness critic caught**, and the adversarial reviewers verified the catch on disk: the
OSSYS basis is `[module; entity]` and ReadSide's is `[schema; table]` — *different namespaces* — so segmentation
alone cannot make a recovered key equal an authored one, and `PhysicalSchema.fs:44` explicitly does **not**
compare SsKeys. ReadSide-recovered identity is *deliberately a separate IR domain* (you recover a schema you did
not author; its identities are not supposed to alias OSSYS-authored ones — the sanctioned bridge for schemas you
*did* author is the V2.SsKey extended-property recovery, which covers **kinds**). So the unanimous "fix" was a
regression in disguise. The synthesis **downgrades it from a recommendation to an open adjudication**: the real
T-I question is whether *authored-schema attribute* round-trip via the V2.SsKey extended property holds today
(`recoverKindSsKey` covers kinds, not attributes) — and *if* it fails for attributes, the fix is per-attribute
extended-property emission, **not** changing the flat fallback's segmentation. This is the single clearest
demonstration in the whole study of *why* the adversarial layer earns its cost: it stopped four converging voices
from writing a regression into the masterwork.

### 3.4 The dedup mandate

The reviewers noted that the same primitive was recommended four-to-seven times under different lens framings
(the Decision-axis FK-trust field appeared in ten distinct recommendations; the digest in four; the trigger-skip
in three; the surrogate struct in three). Convergence is signal — but the *synthesis* must dedupe to **one
canonical move per primitive**, or the roadmap inflates a single facet into a dozen tasks. §6 does exactly this:
every canonical move below is the deduplicated representative of its convergence cluster, scored by the
adversarial panel, and carries its kills and revisions inline.

## 4. The algebra deepened, the IR compressed

This is the intellectual core: where *more* algebra frees an invariant or collapses code, and where N parallel
implementations are secretly one fold over a typed intermediate shape. The governing constraint is strict and
the codebase enforces it on itself: **no structure pays rent in elegance.** It must compress real code or free a
real invariant, and its forcing function must have fired. Several tempting moves fail that test and are recorded
here as *correctly dead*, because naming what not to build is half the discipline.

### 4.1 The descriptor wants to become data

The engine has already internalized "nine capabilities, one law" *philosophically*. The remaining work is to
internalize it at the *implementation* level — and the codebase is unusually far along: `ArtifactByKind.perKind`
collapsed the six emitters' per-kind walk; `Composition.fanOut` collapsed the four tightening strategies'
traversal; `ChannelDiff<'change>` collapsed four byte-identical diff records into one carrier;
`RegisteredTransforms.chainSteps` collapsed three parallel pass-lists into one definition site. So the thesis is
not "the engine is full of copy-paste." It is the sharper, second-order observation:

> **The engine keeps collapsing the *traversal* while leaving the *descriptor* duplicated.** `fanOut` unified the
> walk but left four near-identical pass *files*; `perKind` unified the per-kind map but left two byte-identical
> `kindJsonNode` *dances*; `ChannelDiff` unified the carrier but left three byte-identical *builders* and three
> apply-patchers.

The move, everywhere it appears, is to push the already-discovered intermediate shape one level up — from "shared
traversal, duplicated descriptor" to "the descriptor IS data, traversed once." Three sites are ripe, with their
forcing functions demonstrably fired:

- **The diff `ChannelSpec`.** Reify the kind-scoped diff channel as a value
  `ChannelSpec<'entity,'facet> = { entitiesOf; keyOf; nameOf; changedFacets; mkChange; applyFacet }`. The four
  `*Diff` builders + four `apply*Diff` patchers + four fold-blocks collapse to one fold per direction over four
  small spec values. The fired trigger is in the source itself: the comment "mirrors `attributeDiff` EXACTLY."
  This is the genuine `applyMove` fold the change ontology asks for, *at the schema-diff grain*.
- **The `JsonDocumentWriter` seam.** The `Utf8JsonWriter → MemoryStream → byte[] → JsonNode.Parse(span)` dance is
  verbatim across `JsonEmitter`, `DistributionsEmitter`, `CatalogCodec`, `ProfileCodec`. One pair of helpers —
  `writeToNode : (Utf8JsonWriter -> unit) -> JsonNode` and a node→indented-string renderer — restores the
  description-first shape for every JSON sibling for free.
- **The binding interpreter.** The nine `*Binding` modules differ only in *which* config field they read, their
  identity default, whether they resolve refs, and their error subcode — all *data*; the control flow is
  identical. One interpreter + nine declarations removes eight copies of `bindError`/`aggregate`/opt-in-gate, and
  collapsing the three divergent bind-convergences into one `bind-all` **structurally closes** the model-read-vs-
  live `applyModuleFilter` divergence the config-control-plane doc names as its riskiest seam.

And one move correctly **deferred**: the four tightening passes (`NullabilityPass`/`UniqueIndexPass`/
`ForeignKeyPass`/`CategoricalUniquenessPass`) are near-identical `fanOut` wrappers, and lifting them into one
`TighteningStrategy` descriptor record *would* turn four ~280-line files into four ~40-line values. But the
forcing function (a fifth intervention, or a divergence between the four) has not fired, so per *IR-grows-under-
evidence* it waits. The deferral is itself a methodology — the discipline of *not* compressing until the second
shape forces the abstraction is what keeps the abstractions honest.

The inverse hunt — places where two algebras were forced into *one* shape and should be **split** — is real but
narrow: `EmitError` and the `SchemaLoss ↔ Statement` `RequireQualifiedAccess` collision are the two named sites.
Compression is not a monotone good; the same judgment that merges four channels splits these two.

### 4.2 The torsor wants its missing arrow

The change algebra is the codebase's deepest realized structure, and it is *almost* a groupoid. `CatalogDiff`
has the metric (`norm`), the partial composition (`compose`, typed by endpoints), and the action (`applyDiff`).
A groupoid is a category in which **every arrow is invertible** — and the one piece never built is the inverse
arrow. This is not a wish; it is the directly-uncashed corollary of T12–T16, and it is nearly free:

```fsharp
let inverse (d: CatalogDiff) : CatalogDiff = between (target d) (source d)   // the endpoints are stored fields
```

Three things fall out of those ~3 lines. First, a **freed property**:
`applyDiff (inverse d) (target d) = source d` — the rollback witness the engine *cannot currently state*.
Second, the **groupoid law** `compose d (inverse d) = identity-at-source` becomes a green test, completing the
algebra's defining structure. Third — and this is the connection the chorus analyzed only in halves — the

absent inverse (an algebraic concern) are **the same gap viewed twice** — and naming them as one is the kind of
